About Rhetoric University

A place for innovation, ingenuity, and inspiration.

Enjoy the balmy town of Mason, Ohio, home to the main campus of Rhetoric University. At Rhetoric University, we believe in universal education for all--- that's why we offer free admission to all hopeful rhetoricians that enter through our gates. As you walk through downtown Mason, feel free to enjoy a pizza at the luxurious Two Cities, or hang up a hammock next to the Mason Public Library and enjoy a nice book. If you don't like the weather, don't worry--- just wait five minutes, and it's sure to change.

History of Rhetoric University

Rhetoric University is a public university that offers a unique culture and fulfilling academics. Rhetoric University was founded in Greece by Aristotle and later brought to the Americas by John Downe’s wife Sukey. Here at RU we take great pride in our energetic and welcoming culture. Our Mascot, the ugly troll doll (AKA the Gellar Cup) reflects this culture. Rhetoric University has satellite campuses located in Harry Potter World, the Grand Caymen Islands, and Aristotle’s Greek Isles. Founded by the CompGoddesses and inspired by the brilliance of Aristotle, Plato, and the cast of Friends, we are a university dedicated to what you say and how you say it. With such illustrious alumni like F. Scott Fitzgerald, Lucile Vaughn Payne, and a reincarnated Joan of Arc, Rhetoric University is the epitome of excellent academics, ferocious competition, and superb, gluten-free treats.
